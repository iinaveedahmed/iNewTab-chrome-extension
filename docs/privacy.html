Privacy Policy

Last Updated: December 2024

TL;DR: iNewTab does not collect, store, or transmit any of your personal data to our servers. We don't have servers. All your data stays on your device and syncs directly with Google using OAuth 2.0.

1. Introduction

iNewTab ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how our Chrome extension handles your information. Unlike many other services, we've designed iNewTab to work without collecting any of your personal data.

2. Information We DO NOT Collect

iNewTab does not collect, store, or transmit any personal information to our servers. Specifically, we do not collect:

  • Personal identification information (name, email, address, phone number)
  • Browsing history or web activity
  • Search queries or search history
  • Task content or to-do list data
  • Location data beyond what's required for weather display
  • Analytics or usage statistics
  • IP addresses
  • Cookie data
  • Device information

3. How Your Data is Stored

Local Storage

All your data is stored locally on your device using Chrome's built-in storage API. This includes:

  • Tasks: Your to-do list items, descriptions, due dates, and completion status
  • Settings: Your preferences for theme, visible widgets, and RSS feeds
  • Cached Data: Temporary storage of news articles, weather data, and quotes for offline access
  • Authentication Tokens: OAuth tokens for Google Tasks (stored securely by Chrome)

Google Tasks Synchronization

When you choose to connect your Google account, iNewTab uses OAuth 2.0 to sync your tasks directly with Google Tasks. This connection:

  • Is completely optional - you can use iNewTab without connecting Google
  • Uses Google's official OAuth 2.0 protocol for secure authentication
  • Sends data directly from your browser to Google's servers
  • Does not pass through any intermediary servers
  • Only requests access to Google Tasks (not your Gmail, Drive, or other Google services)

4. Third-Party Services

iNewTab uses the following third-party services, which have their own privacy policies:

Google Tasks API

When you authenticate with Google Tasks:

  • Your tasks sync directly with Google's servers
  • Google's privacy policy applies to this data
  • View Google's Privacy Policy at: https://policies.google.com/privacy

Open-Meteo Weather API

For weather information:

  • We use your approximate location (city-level) to fetch weather data
  • Location data is sent directly to Open-Meteo's API
  • No personal identification is sent
  • Open-Meteo is a free, open-source weather API

RSS2JSON API

For news feeds:

  • RSS feed URLs you configure are processed to display news
  • No personal data is sent to this service
  • Only the RSS feed URLs are transmitted

DummyJSON API

For inspirational quotes:

  • Anonymous requests are made to fetch quotes
  • No personal information is transmitted
  • Quotes are cached locally for offline use

5. Permissions Explained

iNewTab requests the following Chrome permissions:

Storage

Why: To save your tasks, settings, and cached data locally on your device.

What we access: Chrome's local storage API (no remote servers involved).

Identity

Why: To enable OAuth 2.0 authentication with Google Tasks.

What we access: Google's authentication flow (no access to your identity data).

Google Tasks API Scope

Why: To read and write your tasks when you enable Google Tasks sync.

What we access: Only your Google Tasks data, nothing else from your Google account.

6. Data Security

We take security seriously, even though we don't store your data:

  • OAuth 2.0: Industry-standard secure authentication
  • HTTPS Only: All API communications use encrypted connections
  • Content Security Policy: Enforced to prevent XSS attacks
  • XSS Prevention: All user input is properly escaped
  • Minimal Permissions: We only request the bare minimum permissions needed
  • No Tracking: No analytics, no tracking pixels, no third-party scripts

7. Your Rights and Controls

Data Deletion

You have complete control over your data:

  • Uninstall the extension to remove all local data
  • Disconnect from Google Tasks in settings to revoke access
  • Clear individual tasks or settings as needed
  • All data is stored locally and can be removed instantly

Google Account Access

You can revoke iNewTab's access to your Google account at any time:

  • Visit Google Account Permissions
  • Find iNewTab in the list of apps
  • Click "Remove Access"

8. Children's Privacy

iNewTab does not knowingly collect data from anyone, including children under 13. Since we don't collect any data at all, the service is safe for users of all ages. However, Google account integration requires users to meet Google's age requirements for account ownership.

9. Open Source Transparency

iNewTab is fully open source under the MIT License. You can:

  • Review our entire source code on GitHub
  • Audit our privacy and security practices yourself
  • Verify that we don't collect or transmit personal data
  • Fork and modify the extension for your own use

GitHub Repository: https://github.com/iinaveedahmed/iNewTab-chrome-extension

10. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be:

  • Posted on this page with an updated "Last Updated" date
  • Announced in the extension's release notes
  • Published in the GitHub repository

We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy periodically for any changes.

11. Contact Us

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy or iNewTab's privacy practices, please:

  • Open an issue on GitHub: GitHub Issues
  • Start a discussion: GitHub Discussions

Summary: Your privacy is paramount. iNewTab is designed from the ground up to respect your data. We don't collect it, we don't store it on our servers (we don't have servers!), and we don't sell it. Your data stays on your device and, if you choose, syncs directly with Google using industry-standard OAuth 2.0.
