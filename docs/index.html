help

Frequently Asked Questions

How does iNewTab sync with Google Tasks?

iNewTab uses OAuth 2.0 to connect directly to Google Tasks API. There's no middle server - your data flows securely between your browser and Google's servers. Syncing happens automatically every 5 minutes, and you can also manually sync anytime.

Is my data secure?

Absolutely! Your data never passes through our servers. All communication happens directly between your browser and Google using OAuth 2.0. Data is stored locally in Chrome storage and only syncs with your Google account.

Can I use this extension offline?

Yes! All your tasks and settings are stored locally in Chrome storage. You can view and edit tasks offline, and they'll automatically sync with Google Tasks when you're back online.

How do I switch between different Google Task Lists?

Open Settings → Google tab, and you'll see a dropdown with all your Google Task Lists. Select the list you want to display, and it will immediately load those tasks.

Does drag-and-drop reordering sync with Google Tasks?

Yes! When you reorder tasks using drag-and-drop, the new order syncs back to Google Tasks using the native position-based API, ensuring consistency across all your devices.

What permissions does the extension need?

iNewTab only requests the minimum necessary permissions: storage (for local data), identity (for OAuth), and Google Tasks API scope. We never request access to browsing history or other sensitive data.

How do I customize which widgets are visible?

Hover over the top-right corner to reveal the settings icon. In settings, you can toggle visibility for greeting, quote, clock, search, tasks, weather, and news widgets. Hidden widgets don't load or consume resources.

Can I add my own RSS feeds?

Yes! Go to Settings → News tab, where you can add or remove RSS feed URLs. Your custom feeds will be saved and used for the news ticker.

What search engines are supported?

iNewTab supports 8 search engines: Perplexity (default), Google, ChatGPT, DuckDuckGo, Bing, YouTube, GitHub, and Stack Overflow. Each has a keyboard shortcut (e.g., g + Tab for Google).

Is iNewTab really free?

Yes, 100% free! iNewTab is open source under the MIT License. There are no premium features, no subscriptions, and no hidden costs. You can even fork it and customize it for your needs.

How do I contribute to the project?

We welcome contributions! Visit our GitHub repository, fork the project, make your changes, and submit a pull request. You can also report issues or suggest features.

What browsers are supported?

iNewTab works on Chrome 88+, Edge 88+ (Chromium-based), Brave Browser, and any Chromium-based browser with extension support.
